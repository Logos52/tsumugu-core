title: "A Component Changes Jobs"
date: 2026-07-02
description: "A component's role, meaning, sound, or nothing, is assigned character by character; one shape, many jobs, worked through 立."
---

A learner who has broken down a few dozen characters usually settles into a comfortable assumption: each component has a job, and once its job is known, it can be trusted to do that job everywhere. The piece that means water means water, the piece that says tang says tang, and decomposition becomes a lookup table.

The script is not built that way. A component's role is assigned character by character, and the same shape can hold a different job in every character it enters. 立 (lì, "to stand") makes the point almost by itself. In 端 (duān, "to stand up straight") it contributes its meaning. In 拉 (lā, "to pull") and 粒 (lì, "grain") it contributes its sound. In 位 (wèi, "position") it contributes both at once. In 音 (yīn, "sound") it contributes nothing at all, an empty shape left behind by the glyph's history. One component, four different jobs, and no way to know which from the shape alone. Every new character asks for a fresh classification.

| Character | 立's job inside it |
| --- | --- |
| 端 duān | Meaning |
| 拉 lā, 粒 lì | Sound |
| 位 wèi | Meaning and sound at once |
| 音 yīn | Nothing: an empty shape |

The classification matters more than it looks, because errors cascade. A component misread as a meaning carrier invites a story that its siblings will not support. A component misread as a sound carrier corrupts the sound families built on it later: 古 (gǔ) feeds the g, k, h initials in 故, 估, 苦, and 胡 but the j, q, x initials in 居 and 鋸, and a learner who filed it under one group meets the other as chaos. The single decision at first contact quietly sets the quality of everything stacked on top of it.

One warning belongs inside this rule: the radical is not the answer to the role question. Radicals were invented to file characters into dictionary sections, and filing is still their one dependable job. Among six common characters containing 立, it is the radical in only two, and in 章 the radical is simultaneously non-functional. In 位, where 立 does double duty as meaning and sound, the assigned radical is 亻, a different component entirely. Asking the radical which part explains the character feeds a filing fact into a structure question, and the answers come back wrong often enough to mislead even published scholars.

**The radical is a filing address, not an explanation.**

The cost of per-character classification is honest work at first contact: a moment of asking which part carries the sound, which the meaning, and whether either claim actually checks out against the character's reading and sense. It goes faster with practice, because roles have tendencies, some components almost always play sound, others almost always meaning, and the tendencies prune the guesses. But a tendency is a prior, not a verdict, and the verdict stays per character.

New characters keep arriving with the same familiar pieces inside them, and the pieces keep changing jobs. What per-character classification changes is what a familiar piece is worth. Instead of one memorized answer applied everywhere and wrong at the edges, each appearance gets read for what it is doing here, and the component knowledge compounds into a system that predicts instead of one that guesses.
