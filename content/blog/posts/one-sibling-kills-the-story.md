title: "One Sibling Kills the Story"
date: 2026-07-02
description: "A component story is a claim about every character that contains the component, and one sibling that can't absorb it kills it."
---

Character mnemonics are everywhere in the Chinese-learning world, and the popular ones are vivid. A widely shared story for 偷 (tōu, "to steal") reads the character as a person 亻 climbing onto someone's rooftop by night, knife in hand. It is memorable, it seems to fit the parts, and thousands of learners carry it. The trouble starts with the character next door.

A component is not private to one character. The same piece recurs across a whole family of characters, which means a story about what a component depicts is not a story about one character at all. It is a claim about every character in the family, and the family gets a vote. The piece under 偷's roof-and-knife reading is 俞, and 俞 also sits inside 輸 (shū, "to transport; to lose"), 愈 (yù, "to heal"), 愉 (yú, "delighted"), and 喻 (yù, "to explain; analogy"). None of them absorbs a rooftop, a night, or a knife. Four siblings, four failures. The real structure is plainer: 俞 carries the sound, 亻 carries the meaning, and the sense "to steal" extended out of an original "degenerate, insincere" through "secretly."

```text
the story:    偷 = a person on a rooftop at night, knife in hand
the siblings: 俞 also sits in 輸 transport · 愈 heal · 愉 delighted · 喻 explain
the vote:     no roofs, no nights, no knives → the story dies
the parse:    俞 sound + 亻 meaning; "degenerate" → "secretly" → "steal"
```

This is the test, and it costs about a minute: before keeping any story about a component, hold it up against every known character containing that component. A story the whole family can absorb is probably tracking something real. A story that only works in one character is decoration stapled to that character, and it tends to charge interest later, because a false component reading exports damage. Reading the shape 亼 as a roof makes three other characters harder, since its actual identity, a mouth speaking downward from above, is what unlocks the original senses of 合 ("to answer"), 命 ("to give orders"), and 令 ("command"). One bad reading degrades its whole cluster.

**One sibling that cannot absorb the story kills it.**

The test does not ban stories. Memory loves a scene, and a scene built on the real analysis reinforces every character it touches: learn the true structure first, then decorate it. What the test bans is trusting a story because it is vivid, which is precisely the property that spreads bad ones. Internet breakdowns earn default suspicion for this reason; many carve characters into pieces that carry no function at all and hang stories on the fragments.

There is a floor to respect. The test needs siblings to run against, so early on, with a small character inventory, most stories cannot be checked yet, and holding them loosely is the honest position. And the test can only falsify; passing it is evidence, not proof, since a family of two or three might absorb a wrong story by luck.

New mnemonics will keep arriving as long as characters get learned, and the vivid ones will keep being tempting. What the sibling test changes is which ones get to stay. The stories that survive a family audit are the ones tracking the script's real machinery, and those pay their rent everywhere the component appears.
