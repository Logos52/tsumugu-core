# Supplementary articles — ideas for Opus to author

Beyond each lesson's **3 core** readings (union = 100% of the lesson's vocab +
grammar), add **~6 supplementary** articles per lesson: same level, same
vocab/grammar reinforced, but new scenes and genres — so the library is deep and
*interesting* to browse, not a thin 3-per-lesson list. Every article carries an
evocative title (the interest landmark) and, later, a cover.

The multiplier is a **recurring cast**: supplementary articles are episodes in an
ongoing world, so browsing feels like following a series.

---

## The cast
The recurring cast — **six international students learning Chinese in Taiwan**, plus
the teacher, the noodle-shop owner, and a cat — is the canon in **[`CAST.md`](./CAST.md)**.
Read it there; the articles and the covers both build from it. (Supersedes the earlier
local-Taiwanese ensemble.)

## The angle menu (rotate these for a lesson's 6 supplementary articles)
Each reinforces the *same* lesson vocab + grammar at the *same* level, in a new shape:
1. **POV flip** — retell a core scene through another character's eyes (自述 / 短文).
2. **Diary / message thread** — a 日記 entry or a 訊息 text exchange.
3. **Real Taiwan document** — a notice, menu, schedule, flyer, or ad at level (真實語境).
4. **Broadcast / report** — a 廣播 segment or 報導 on the lesson's topic.
5. **Arc episode** — a small story that moves the cast forward (a mishap, a plan, a reunion).
6. **Compare / opinion / how-to** — two characters weigh options, or a how-to, using the topic's structures.

*(These map onto the format signifier icons: dialogue · self · story · report · broadcast · diary.)*

## Premise bank by level (seeds to draw from)
- **A1 入門** — first meetings, names, nationalities, numbers, simple wants:
  orientation introductions, ordering bubble tea, buying a SIM card, asking the way
  to the MRT, "which one is cheaper," meeting the cast.
- **A2 基礎** — daily city life, shopping, health, plans: the garbage truck, the night
  market, seeing a doctor, a weekend day-trip, sending a parcel, phone calls, weather.
- **B1 進階** — school / work, relationships, opinions, mild conflict: starting
  university, a part-time job, roommate friction, an awkward date, a lost wallet,
  family expectations, moving flats.
- **B2 高階** — society, technology, arts, culture, longer narratives: online life, an
  art exhibit, cloud tech at work, feng-shui and where to put the bed, a viral clip.
- **C1 流利** — abstract / opinion, media, society: a neighbourhood gentrifying,
  work-life-balance debate, a documentary, social-media ethics, a job interview.
- **C2 精通** — nuanced / argumentative / literary: an op-ed, a short literary vignette,
  a philosophical dialogue between two of the cast, gentle satire.

## Authoring rules (carry over from the companion gate)
- Stay **in level**: reuse/reinforce the lesson's vocab + grammar; out-of-band only
  as advisory, naturalness first.
- **Original**: invented cast + scenes; never the ACCC textbook cast or dialogues.
- Each article ships with **title_zh / title_en / title_vi** + a one-line premise (the
  card hook) — see `TITLE-PROMPT-COMPOSER.md`.
- Traditional Chinese; Taiwan-native detail, never costume.
